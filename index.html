Poetry and manliness

Venezuela demands old-school masculinity.

Likes Poetry? Gay.

Not tall? Gay.

Likes Ballet? Super Gay.

So I was super gay, super bullied, super using videogames as an escape mechanism.

But at 25 years old I flew off to Colombia to study literature.

There I discovered a masculinity granted by the Great Feminine.

Giving yourself permission to feel vulnerable, to feel pain, is the only way to find healing.

In Bill "Superfoot" Wallace's words: "The guy who said no pain, no gain... He's DEAD! Let it heal! Let it rest!"

Boxing and inner Demons

Almost a decade later, Ex-world Champion Miguel Angel Castellini , would walk into my life.

It was at his gym in Argentina, where the much taller fighters of European ancestry were destroying me.

Castellini needs to watch only one of my fights.

He tells me, with the sweetness of an old father figure, that my stance is too wide to chase taller fighters.

That I needed a footwork like Joe Frazier's.

And so Castellini sends me down a research path of boxing history that would change how I face the ghosts of my bullies in the ring, and how I feel about myself, forever.

Programming and a Happy ending

I started programming at 35 to get out of dead-end jobs teaching and freelancing.

I came for the money but I stayed because programming is an art.

Writing code feels like writing prose, designing feels like painting on a canvas.

Unexpectedly, programming helped me develop a new, healthy relationship with computers, away from videogames.

So here we are at the end.

At the edge of a new beginning.

Thank you, people at Harvard, for what you do and put out there for free.

There are many of us who could never have afforded this.

To us this means the world.

Thank you.
